# About

My name is "Tiddy Hunter", creator of the Tiddy Hunter channel and the Libertad Research Archive.

What began as a personal interest in silver during the aftermath of the 2008 financial crisis gradually evolved into a deeper fascination with monetary systems, inflation, market structure, and eventually the world of Mexican Libertads and modern numismatics.

Over time, I became increasingly drawn to areas of the market where historical context, scarcity, and data were often poorly documented or overlooked entirely. That interest ultimately led me toward researching Libertads, particularly low-mintage and underappreciated modern Mexican issues.

## Why Libertads

For many years, I was primarily a stacker of world bullion coins. Libertads stood apart.

Unlike many modern bullion programs, the Libertad series developed a unique combination of:
- limited mintages
- irregular production
- collector-driven scarcity
- historical inconsistency in documentation
- and unusually strong long-term collector demand

As I spent more time studying the series, I realized that many aspects of the market lacked centralized research, particularly regarding:
- surviving population estimates
- grading population trends
- historical availability
- mintage inconsistencies
- and long-term collector behavior

This archive was created to help document and preserve that information.

## Research Philosophy

The goal of this site is not hype or short-term speculation.

I believe meaningful analysis comes from combining:
- historical context
- original data collection
- population tracking
- macroeconomic understanding
- and long-term perspective

Many of the charts, population reports, survival estimates, and market studies presented here are built from independently gathered data, including ongoing tracking of NGC and PCGS population changes.

Where possible, I try to prioritize transparency, context, and evidence over sensationalism.

## What You’ll Find Here

This archive contains:
- Libertad population analysis
- grading census tracking
- survival estimate modeling
- historical market commentary
- Pre-Columbian series research
- macroeconomic essays
- companion articles for YouTube videos
- downloadable charts and datasets

## Long-Term Goal

The long-term objective is to build a living research archive dedicated to Libertads, modern Mexican numismatics, and broader precious metals analysis.

In an era where online information is increasingly fragmented and temporary, I believe there is value in building something archival, searchable, and continuously refined over time.
